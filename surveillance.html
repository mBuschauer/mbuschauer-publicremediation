Our Privacy

China's WeChat and the NSA's Whistleblower

In vast digital realm China controls, WeChat stands as its a undisputed king of social apps. It is woven into every part of daily life from taxes to gaming. Yet, it serves as a powerful tool for surveillance and censorship. A 2020 report by the Citizen Lab revealed how WeChat serves as a sophisticated method for monitoring images and files - even those shared by non-China-registered accounts. Surveilled contents are further used to refine censorship algorithms that targeting China-registered users, operating under a veil of secrecy with no transparency to its users.

In 2013, Edward Snowden's revelations jolted the world when he revealed the extent of the NSA's domestic surveillance. His disclosures unveiled a vast network of data collection, including a program compelling telecom companies like Verizon to hand over US citizens' call records and PRISM, a scheme that saw the NSA collaborating with tech giants to access online communications. This revealed how the NSA, often working from the shadows, had been systematically collecting massive amounts of data, blurring the lines of privacy and security.
