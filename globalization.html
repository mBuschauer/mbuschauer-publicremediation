Russia's Information Campaign During the War in Ukraine

Narrative Control as a Weapon

In the shadows of the internet, Russia's "Informatsionnaya voyna" (information war) unfolds as a multi-pronged strategy, far beyond mere cyberwarfare. This digital crusade encompasses controlling undersea communication cables, sculpting national narratives, and manipulating the news cycle, all while flooding the information space with an army of bots and trolls. The objective? To achieve strategic victory and reflexive control. In Russia, a tight grip on media allows the state to sway public opinion effectively. The Kremlin's narrative, consistently broadcasted for years, frames its actions in Ukraine as a protective measure against so-called Ukrainian Nazis, a claim that resonates with a significant portion of the Russian populace. This deep-rooted narrative strategy, built on repetition and familiarity, has been particularly potent during the escalation of the war in Ukraine in 2022.

Russia's information war against Ukraine has been a relentless endeavor, especially highlighted under the pro-Russian leadership of President Victor Yanukovych. A striking example of this deceptive narrative occurred in February 2014. Amidst military maneuvers that signaled an ominous future, Russia steadfastly denied any aggressive intentions. The Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs dismissed the notion of the Black Sea Fleet's involvement in destabilizing Crimea. Further, President Putin casually referred to the armed men, who forcefully tried to seize the Crimean parliament, as mere "local self-defense forces." This calculated denial and reframing of aggressive actions under the guise of peace and self-defense have been a cornerstone of Russia's information strategy against Ukraine.
