Facebook's Role in the Myanmar Genocide

How Algorithms and Shape how we Think

Facebook's role in the Myanmar genocide is tragic example of how social media can contribute to real-world consequences and, in this case, violence. In 2017, the Myanmar military launched a campaign against the Rohingya, a predominantly Muslim ethnic minority in Myanmar's northern Rakhine State. This campaign involved systematic murder, rape, and the burning of homes. It led to over 700,000 Rohingya fleeing the region. In the months leading up to this event, Facebook's algorithms amplified hatred and helped incite violence against the Rohingya. The platform, designed to boost engagement, found that promoting controversial topcis was incredible for increasing engagement. Over time, it became an echo chamber for anti-Rohingya content, filled with inflammatory posts that called for violence, hostility, and discrimination against the Rohignya.

Despite warnings from local civil society activists and internal research that found Facebook's algorithms could cause real-world harm, Meta failed to take appropriate actions. Studies continue to find that Meta consistently failed to enforce its policies on hate speech. The United Nations' Independent International Fact-Finding Mission on Myanmar concluded that social media played a significant role in the atrocities, underscoring the devastating impact Facebook had on the Rohingya community. During the lead-up to the genocide, Meta only employed 2 burmese-speaking content moderators to moderate over 7.1 million Burmese people. Internal studies at Meta, dating back to 2012, indicated that Meta was aware that its algorithms could result real-world harm. Specifically, a 2016 study by Meta, clearly acknowledged that “our recommendation systems grow the problem” of extremism. In an internal document dated August 2019, a Meta employee wrote: “We have evidence from a variety of sources that hate speech, divisive political speech, and misinformation on Facebook… are affecting societies around the world. We also have compelling evidence that our core product mechanics, such as virality, recommendations, and optimizing for engagement, are a significant part of why these types of speech flourish on the platform.”

In an age where digital privacy and regulation are paramount, the complex algorithms of social media platforms, such as Facebook and YouTube, demand closer scrutiny. Recent studies have illuminated how these algorithms can create ideological echo chambers, subtly shaping political discourse and reinforcing biases. While they significantly influence what content users see and interact with, their impact on changing political beliefs is less clear. This highlights the dual challenge in the digital era: ensuring that social media algorithms foster a balanced information landscape while safeguarding user privacy and data. As we navigate this terrain, the need for transparent and responsible algorithmic regulation becomes increasingly critical, not just for protecting individual privacy, but also for preserving the integrity of our digital public square.
